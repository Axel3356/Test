📦 How to Prepare for Your Move

A little preparation before the move saves you significant time — and since time is money here, these tips can directly reduce your bill. Here’s what actually makes a difference:

🔧 Disassemble Furniture Where Possible

This is the single most impactful thing you can do. Consider a wardrobe or cabinet that is 180 cm long × 100 cm wide × 50 cm deep. Loaded whole, it fills roughly 80% of the available cargo space — leaving almost no room for anything else and likely requiring a second trip. Disassemble it into its main panels (top, sides, shelves) and the same wardrobe uses around 20% of the space, flat against the wall of the cargo area. Everything else fits around it. One trip instead of two — and you save an hour.

The same logic applies to bed frames: remove the legs, bundle the slats, and the frame becomes flat and easy to slide in. A few minutes with an allen key saves real money.

💡 Tip: Keep a small bag with all screws, bolts and allen keys from disassembled furniture. Label it with a piece of tape. Reassembly becomes much faster.

📦 Pack Boxes Smart

Heavy items (books, kitchen appliances) go in small boxes — they stay manageable to lift. Light bulky items (pillows, linens, clothing) go in larger boxes or bags. Overfilled boxes collapse and slow everything down.

Fill boxes fully — partially filled boxes crush when stacked. If a box feels too light, add clothing or towels to fill the space.

🏭 Recycling Centre Runs — What to Prepare

If you’re booking a trip to the Recyclinghof or Entsorgungsstelle, separate your items beforehand by material type (metal, wood, electronics, general waste). Most recycling centres in Zurich require sorted drop-offs, and pre-sorting means we spend less time there — which saves you money.

📱 Electronics and Cables

Before unplugging your TV, monitor setup, or home office, take a quick photo of the cable connections at the back. When you rebuild at the new place, the photo does the thinking for you.

Coil cables and secure them with a rubber band or cable tie. Loose cables tangle and take time to sort out.

🔒 Wrap Fragile Items With What You Already Have

You don’t need to buy bubble wrap. Wrap glasses and fragile items in clothing, towels, or scarves. They protect just as well, and you’re packing two things at once.

👥 If You Have Pets

Pets are welcome to travel in the vehicle during the move. However, if your cat or dog tends to get anxious around loading activity, it helps to keep them in a quiet room while boxes are being moved, then bring them to the vehicle last. They settle much faster when the chaos is already over.
